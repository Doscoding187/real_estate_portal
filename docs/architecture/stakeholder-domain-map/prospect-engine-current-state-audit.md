# Prospect Engine Current-State Audit

Status: documentation-only architecture audit
Audited branch/commit: `docs/prospect-engine-architecture` / `f18ec486064a728e40dcb70016683cf864554c0c`
Scope: public prospect journey, its persistence, and its hand-off to agency, development, distribution, and services workflows. “Complete” below means the route, callable server boundary, persistence and intended ownership are all present; it does not mean the broader Prospect Engine exists.

## Executive finding

Property Listify has strong public discovery, a real authenticated saved-search flow, public lead capture, development lead routing, and agency-side buyer lead/viewing operations. It does **not** yet have one coherent prospect engine. Several older prospect components call an unregistered `trpc.prospects.*` router; favourites, saved items, recent viewing and comparison have separate implementations; and public lead rows have no authenticated prospect identity link. The first implementation must join existing records rather than introduce a parallel prospect CRM.

## Existing operational workflow that must be reused

`leads` is the current public lead intake aggregate. It stores property/development, agency/agent, contact snapshot, status, UTM fields, lead source, follow-up fields, qualification data, and private notes. `agency.getLeads`, `getLeadDetail`, assignment, status/contact/follow-up procedures and `AgencyLeadsWorkspace` operate it. `agency.scheduleLeadViewing` creates the canonical agency `showings` record; `AgencyViewingsWorkspace` and My Day manage confirmation, completion, feedback and follow-up. `agency_deals` later links a lead and/or showing into offers and transactions.

The Prospect Engine must add a prospect identity link and a public-safe projection to these aggregates. It must not mirror a public enquiry into `listing_leads`, `scheduled_viewings`, `listing_viewings`, and a new prospect table.

## Evidence and gaps to protect

- `drizzle/schema/core.ts`, `leads.ts`, `listings.ts`, `servicesEngine.ts`, `referrals.ts` and `distribution.ts` establish the separate existing ownership boundaries.
- `server/routers.ts` registers `savedSearch`, `leads`, `agency`, `distribution`, `servicesEngine`, `explore` and `favorites`, but not `prospects`.
- `server/favoritesRouter.ts` and `server/exploreRouter.ts#getSavedProperties` are explicit no-op/stub paths; do not describe them as persistence.
- Development qualification and property contact components currently permit sensitive affordability detail to be attached to a general `leads.affordabilityData` field. The proposed foundation supersedes that pattern; it does not expand it.
