## Accepted evidence boundary for proposition architecture

AAE-S1B may use only the following evidence types, with their limitations stated in the resulting proposition work:

1. Consumer discovery, property-detail, location-insight, saved-search, Explore and affordability foundations are **qualified**, not evidence of coverage, accuracy, personalisation or finance approval.
2. Public enquiry capture is **delivered** as a narrow capability: a supported public enquiry can be captured and routed. It is not evidence of verified, qualified, exclusive, high-volume or guaranteed leads.
3. Agent, agency, developer, provider, finance and distribution workspace/service foundations are **qualified**. They may support cautious workflow language, never performance, adoption, availability, entitlement or outcome guarantees.
4. Release-matched product demonstrations are **qualified proof** only. They can show an existing workflow, not customer results, traction, verification, plan inclusion or commercial availability.
5. Development milestones, paid visibility and syndication remain **exploratory**; lender marketplace, named CRM integration, provider verification/booking and multi-office administration are not marketable.

## Public-implementation blockers

The following block public commercial implementation, route promotion, pricing presentation and launch copy:

1. P0 claims on `/advertise` and `/advertise/sell/agents`: unsubstantiated audience scale, verification, AI, lead quality, pay-for-performance, buyer volume, ID verification, CRM integration, testimonial, ROI, trial, comparative and local-activity claims.
2. P0 pricing/offer claims: 14-day trial, no-card, locked-in pricing, price cards, discounts and launch offers lack a canonical commercial authority.
3. P1 journeys: agent onboarding is browser-local with no verified activation/payment destination; get-started and strategy flows have no evidenced commercial handoff; five audience routes are literal placeholders.
4. P1 content authority: localStorage/sessionStorage advertise CMS and onboarding state cannot govern approved content, eligibility, price, trial or activation.
5. Evidence gaps: no approved metric, testimonial, customer-logo, verification, compliance, price, trial, offer, comparative or case-study evidence is available for public use.

## Commercial-authority blockers

AAE-S1C is not authorised to begin by this pack. If it is separately authorised later, its purpose is to reconcile commercial motions, offers and the conflicts below. Public pricing, offers, trials, eligibility and implementation remain blocked until that architecture has been reviewed and approved. AAE-S2 remains blocked from public implementation until the same conditions are met.

The unresolved conflicts are:

- `plans`/`planEntitlements`/`subscriptions` and `planAccessService`;
- agent entitlement and listing-publication entitlement services;
- agency billing foundation, invoices and coupon snapshots;
- legacy `subscription_plans`/`user_subscriptions` service;
- developer and partner subscription services;
- manual discount/coupon behaviour;
- trial definitions, expiry/cancellation policy, activation, payment/checkout and public eligibility read model;
- browser-local advertise prices/trials/content, which are expressly not authority.

## Evidence owners still unassigned

The repository does not establish accountable owners for product analytics/metrics, compliance verification, public data methodology, finance/legal review, testimonials/customer consent, marketing claims, technology integrations, offer approval and sales handoff. AAE-S1B may name the required roles but must not imply those roles have approved a claim.

## Gate decision

`AAE_S1B_ENTRY=APPROVED_WITH_BOUNDARIES`

AAE-S1B may begin **only as offline proposition architecture**: it may map audiences, jobs, problems, outcomes, objections, differentiators and the Discovery → Trust → Operations → Progression model using the approved AAE-S0 strategic baseline, delivered capability evidence, and explicitly qualified capability evidence with its recorded limitations. It may use exploratory evidence only as clearly labelled future direction, never as current value. It must cite the relevant AAE-S0 and S1A atomic IDs.

AAE-S1B may **not** approve public copy, performance/traction proof, verification, pricing, trials, offers, entitlement inclusion, self-service purchase, sales SLA, lender marketplace, CRM integration, exclusive allocation, paid visibility, campaign analytics or any public route. S1A does not authorise AAE-S1C to begin; if separately authorised, S1C will reconcile commercial motions, offers, pricing and authority conflicts. Public pricing, offers, trials, eligibility and implementation remain blocked until that resulting S1C architecture is reviewed and approved. AAE-S2 remains blocked on public implementation and blocker remediation.

## Re-entry conditions for AAE-S1B review

Before accepting any S1B output, reviewers must confirm that it:

- cites the approved AAE-S0 baseline together with atomic capability IDs from `11` and proof IDs from `14`;
- contains no prohibited wording from `11`, `12` or `14`;
- records whether each proposal aligns with, extends, amends or supersedes AAE-S0;
- leaves price, plan, offer, trial, entitlement, CTA destination and availability decisions to the relevant authorised phase; and
- does not describe prototype or placeholder routes as commercial journeys.

The AAE index remains unchanged until this completed S1A pack has received review and approval.
