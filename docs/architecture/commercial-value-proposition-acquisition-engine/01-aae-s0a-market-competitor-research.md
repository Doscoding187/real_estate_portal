# AAE-S0A — Market & Competitor Research
### Advertise & Partner Acquisition Engine — Property Listify

Research scope: 8 portals across 5 markets (South Africa, US, Australia, India), mapped against the five commercial audience categories identified in the Phase 0 audit. Sources are public — competitor websites, industry press, and analyst/agent commentary. No proprietary or paid data was used, per the brief.

---

## 1. Agent acquisition

| | Property24 / Private Property (SA) | Zillow Premier Agent (US) | realestate.com.au (AU) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Page hierarchy** | No public self-serve agent page; agent commercial terms are negotiated per account/agency, though private-seller listing flows are public and simple | Dedicated Premier Agent hub, but no public price list — you register interest and get a sales call | Ignite (agent self-service platform) sits behind agent login; public marketing pages exist per product (Audience Maximiser, Front Page) with brochures and preview tools |
| **Main promise** | "SA's #1/#2 portal — the place buyers start their search" | "Be the first agent buyers talk to" | "Reach more buyers, convert more listings" |
| **Products shown** | Standard listing tiers, featured listing boost | Auction-based lead delivery by ZIP/suburb, built-in CRM, "My Agent" branding that locks out competitors on future views, Zillow Flex (pay-per-closed-deal, invite-only) | Tiered listing depth (Standard → Feature → Highlight → Premiere) plus paid add-ons (Audience Maximiser, Front Page, Inspection Boost, Sold Boost) |
| **Pricing visibility** | Not public — regulator-scrutinised, see §6 | Not public at all; "dynamic," described by reviewers as a lack of price transparency | Partially public — tiered listing products often have indicative pricing; add-ons like Audience Maximiser are sold through account managers with a PDF brochure |
| **Proof strategy** | General portal traffic stats, agent testimonials | Volume claims (e.g. most buyers choose the first agent who responds) | Dated, footnoted performance stats — e.g. a named % increase in page views, with a reference table stamped "accurate as of [date]" and a stated data window |
| **CTA / onboarding** | Contact an account manager / sign up as an agency | Request pricing (sales-assisted) | Existing customers self-serve inside Ignite; prospects go through account manager |
| **SEO approach** | Portal-wide SEO dominance; agent-facing pages are not SEO-optimised (low public discoverability) | Consumer SEO carries the brand; agent product pages are largely gated | Consumer SEO dominant; agent product marketing lives on a semi-separate "agent" subdomain/section aimed at trade press, not search traffic |

**Read-across for Property Listify:** the two portals with the strongest agent-facing pages (Zillow, REA Group) both keep hard pricing off the public page for anything beyond a basic listing tier — the pattern is publish the *shape* of the offer, gate the *number*. The one place a competitor publishes a real, dated performance number, it's footnoted and time-stamped rather than a bare superlative — directly relevant to the "unverified metrics" problem flagged in the Kiro audit.

## 2. Agency products

| | Property24/Private Property (SA) | Square Yards — Square Connect (India) | realestate.com.au (AU) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Page hierarchy** | Same account as individual agents, scaled by listing/lead volume; no distinct "agency" page | Square Connect is a **separately branded product**, not a page bolted onto the consumer site — it has its own proposition for "real estate channel partners" | Agencies access the same Ignite platform as individual agents, with agency-level branding/reporting layered on top |
| **Main promise** | Portal reach at agency scale | "India's platform for real estate channel partners" — positioned as infrastructure for brokers, not just an ad slot | Agency branding across a shared campaign budget |
| **Products shown** | Multi-listing packages, agency branding | New-project brokerage access, differential payouts, fast payout claims, resale/rental lead access, adjacent services (loans, interiors) bundled in | Shared Audience Maximiser contracts across all of an agency's listings (bought once, applied to every listing, at a lower per-listing cost than buying individually) |
| **Pricing visibility** | Negotiated, volume-tiered — subject to SA competition-law scrutiny (see §6) | Not publicly listed; described in reviews rather than shown as a rate card | Blended into per-listing/campaign pricing, disclosed to existing customers inside Ignite |
| **Proof strategy** | Case-by-case agency testimonials | Reviewer/marketplace listings (e.g. G2) rather than self-published stats | Same dated stat approach as the agent tier |
| **CTA / onboarding** | Account manager | Apply / partner with the platform | Existing relationship extension (agencies already an Ignite customer) |
| **SEO approach** | None distinct from agent tier | Own domain/brand carries its own SEO equity, separate from the Square Yards consumer site | Folded into agent-tier SEO |

**Read-across:** Square Connect is the clearest example of treating "agency/channel-partner" as a **product with its own brand**, not a bigger version of the agent page. Worth deciding early whether Property Listify's agency tier is really a distinct commercial motion (seat/inventory-based, worth its own identity) or just a volume discount on the agent tier — that's a Group 1 decision the audit flagged as still open, and this is a concrete precedent for the "own product" option.

## 3. Developer marketing

| | Property24 / Private Property (SA — developer listings) | 99acres / Housing.com (India) | NoBroker (India) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Page hierarchy** | Developer listings sit inside the standard listing structure; no distinct developer funnel visible publicly | Dedicated new-project/new-launch sections with project microsites | New-projects city landing pages funnelling into individual project pages |
| **Main promise** | Visibility to active buyers | Housing.com in particular leans on rich visual/trust content for the "under-construction" segment | Direct builder access without broker friction |
| **Products shown** | Standard/featured listing | Detailed floor plans, 3D virtual tours, master-plan views, construction-progress photography; RERA (regulator) registration prominently displayed on listed projects | Direct-to-builder contact, pre-launch booking assistance, paperwork/payment support |
| **Pricing visibility** | Not public | Not public (lead-gen model) | Not public — access-and-facilitation model, not a paid placement rate card |
| **Proof strategy** | Portal scale claims | Regulatory-compliance display (RERA number) functions as trust proof, alongside visual richness | Direct-access framing ("skip the broker") is the proof point rather than stats |
| **CTA / onboarding** | Contact | Express interest / enquire | Register interest, book pre-launch |
| **SEO approach** | General portal SEO | Strong long-tail SEO on "new projects in [city]" and "[project name]" pages | Same city + project long-tail pattern |

**Read-across:** the developer segment's proof strategy is *visual and regulatory*, not stat-driven — construction photos, floor plans, and compliance registration numbers substitute for traction claims a pre-launch platform can't yet make. That's directly usable for Property Listify: a developer page can look credible before you have "500+ partners" simply by showing the actual product (project media, unit inventory, milestone tracking) in detail, plus whatever SA-equivalent compliance markers apply (e.g. NHBRC enrolment, sectional-title registration status) — the local equivalent of RERA display.

## 4. Service-provider marketplaces

South African and mainstream property-portal precedent for this category is thin — most portals treat "services" as an ancillary directory rather than a funded acquisition motion, so the closest comparable pattern comes from adjacent services marketplaces (Thumbtack-style lead marketplaces) referenced in the earlier B2B research, plus NoBroker's own internal precedent:

- **NoBrokerHood** — NoBroker's own society/estate-management product is marketed as a **fully separate product line**, aimed at a different buyer (housing-society management committees) through direct outreach and referral rather than portal SEO. This is the clearest signal in this research set that a "service provider" or adjacent-audience segment often performs better as its own mini-brand with its own channel logic than as a spoke bolted onto the main portal nav.
- Property24/Private Property's own service-adjacent offers (attorney/conveyancing partners, e.g. Snymans) are positioned as **embedded referral partners inside the seller journey**, not as a paid "advertise with us" product at all — the portal refers the lead, not the reverse.

**Read-across:** service providers may be the segment least suited to a standard "/advertise" spoke. The two working precedents both point toward either (a) a lead-referral partnership embedded in a consumer journey (attorney model) or (b) an entirely separate branded product with its own outreach motion (NoBrokerHood model) — not a self-service subscription page. Worth flagging to whoever owns Service Listify's positioning specifically, since it may need its own commercial-model decision rather than inheriting the agent/agency page pattern.

## 5. Banks and bond originators

| | Property24 / Private Property (SA) |
|---|---|
| **Relationship type** | Not an advertiser relationship — **ooba is a disclosed equity investor in Private Property**, and BetterBond operates as an embedded referral/calculator partner across agent and portal journeys |
| **Products shown to consumers** | Affordability and bond calculators embedded directly in the buyer journey (Property24 hosts its own calculators; BetterBond/ooba tools appear via agent and portal partnerships) |
| **Commercial model** | Bond originators are free to the consumer — banks pay the originator for referred business, and the originator's value to the portal is either equity/strategic (ooba↔Private Property) or embedded lead-flow (BetterBond) |
| **Public "pricing" page** | None — there is nothing to "advertise" in the agent-page sense; the relationship is corporate development / partnership, not self-service |

**Read-across:** this confirms the audit's Assumption 4 concern directly. In the one market with a real, documented precedent (SA), the bank/bond-originator relationship isn't a page at all — it's an equity or referral-integration partnership negotiated at the corporate level, surfaced to consumers only as an embedded calculator or "get pre-qualified" widget inside the buyer journey. There's no portal in this research set that runs a public self-service "become our lending partner" landing page. That's a strong signal for keeping this group under a `/partners` or direct business-development track rather than `/advertise`.

## 6. A regulatory data point specific to South Africa

This doesn't map to any single competitor row, but it matters more here than any competitor's marketing copy: South Africa's Competition Commission's Online Intermediation Platforms Market Inquiry found that Property24 and Private Property's agent pricing showed volume-based price discrimination in excess of 300% between small and large agencies with no cost-based justification, and ordered both platforms to introduce capped small-independent-agent packages (SIBP/SIRP), end multi-year lock-in contracts, and stop charging incoming-listing/syndication fees.

**Why this matters for the page architecture, not just the pricing model:** the Commission's remedy explicitly required Property24 to publish a dedicated page explaining who qualifies for the small-agent package and how, with a stated communication channel for those agents. In other words, the SA regulator has already established an expectation that agent-facing commercial pages in this market should be transparent about *eligibility and terms* for smaller players, not just a rate card for large accounts. If Property Listify's agent tier ever reaches meaningful scale in SA, this is a precedent worth designing toward rather than retrofitting under pressure later.

## 7. Cross-cutting patterns (what's now well-supported by more than one market)

1. **Hub-and-spoke, confirmed again** — every multi-segment portal in this set eventually separates its commercial motions into distinct product experiences (Ignite vs. consumer site, Premier Agent vs. Zillow.com, Square Connect vs. Square Yards, NoBrokerHood vs. NoBroker) rather than one page serving everyone.
2. **Self-service pricing is rare, even for agents.** Of the eight portals researched, none publish a full public rate card for their primary agent product. Zillow and REA Group both gate price behind a sales conversation or an existing account. Property24/Private Property's pricing is negotiated and was contested strongly enough to draw a competition-law remedy. This pushes back a little on treating "agents = self-service" as settled — it may still be right for a launch-stage SA platform trying to reduce friction, but it isn't the default the bigger portals converged on.
3. **Higher-tier segments (agencies, developers, financial partners) consistently get their own branded product or their own relationship type**, not a bigger version of the agent page.
4. **Proof substitutes for traction exist and are legitimate** — dated/footnoted performance stats (REA Group), visual and regulatory proof (Housing.com's RERA display, floor plans, construction photography) are both credible ways to build trust before a platform has real scale, without inventing numbers.
5. **Bond originators/banks are a partnership motion, not an advertiser motion**, at least in the one market with a documented example.

## 8. What this research does *not* settle (feeds directly into AAE-S0C)

- Whether Property Listify's agent tier should be self-service (aligns with the friction-reduction argument for a launch-stage platform) or sales-assisted (aligns with what every larger competitor actually does).
- Whether "agency" is a distinct product (Square Connect precedent) or a volume tier on the agent product.
- Where service providers sit — embedded referral vs. standalone branded product — given the thin direct precedent.
- Exact SA-equivalent compliance/trust markers to use on developer pages in place of RERA (NHBRC, sectional title status, etc. — needs its own quick confirmation, not assumed here).
- Whether any future agent-pricing page should be designed with the SIBP/SIRP-style transparency requirement in mind pre-emptively.

These are commercial-model decisions, not page-design decisions, and per the interim verdict this research doesn't attempt to resolve them — it narrows the options with precedent.
