## Purpose

AAE-S1A establishes the evidence boundary that AAE-S1B propositions may use. It does not approve copy, prices, offers, routes, onboarding, CMS work or implementation.

The atomic records in documents 11 through 14 are the working evidence source for this draft. Any orientation summary is expressly derived and non-authoritative. A sentence proposed in later AAE work must be traceable to an atomic capability ID, contradiction ID or proof ID; if it cannot be traced, it is not approved for public use.

## Two independent classifications

| Technical evidence maturity | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Static implementation | Code, route, schema or component exists |
| Test-backed | Focused automated tests verify behaviour |
| Runtime-contract verified | Connected router/service integration is evidenced by contracts or integration tests |
| Operationally evidenced | Configured or end-to-end operation is evidenced |
| Not evidenced | Sufficient implementation evidence was not found |

| Commercial truth status | Public treatment |
| --- | --- |
| Delivered | May be marketed only with approved wording |
| Qualified | May be described only with explicit limitations |
| Approved-next | Approved future delivery; not current-marketable |
| Exploratory | Concept, prototype, placeholder or unapproved direction |
| Prohibited | Must not appear in public claims |

Technical maturity never determines commercial truth by itself.

## Evidence and wording rules

Every register row records audience, customer outcome, platform layer, capability or claim, paths, evidence maturity, commercial status, limitations, permitted wording, prohibited wording, functional owner and freshness requirement.

Evidence types are route/component, router/service, schema/migration, automated test, authority document, operating evidence and external proof. A claim without timely evidence is prohibited until revalidated. Functional ownership must remain unassigned where the repository cannot establish it.

## Claim-type standards

| Claim type | Minimum evidence for public use | Freshness |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Capability | Runtime-contract evidence and named product owner | On release or material change |
| Performance | Instrumented, dated metric and measurement definition | Per reporting period |
| Traction/customer | Source-backed, dated customer/account evidence | Quarterly or campaign expiry |
| Testimonial | Verifiable consent, identity, wording approval and review date | Annual or withdrawal |
| Pricing/offer | Confirmed commercial authority, eligibility and effective date | Every change |
| Availability | Working route, qualification and destination | Every release |
| Compliance/verification | Applicable source and accountable compliance owner | On regulatory change |
| Comparative | Independently supportable, scoped comparator and date | Quarterly |

## Route-readiness rule

Route registration, reachability, navigation exposure, SEO intent, content completeness, CTA functionality, onboarding destination and approved commercial availability are separate fields. A placeholder is never an acquisition journey.

## Required evidence record

For every independently marketable capability, the atomic register records: stable ID; audience; family; customer problem; customer outcome; Discovery, Trust, Operations or Progression layer; observable behaviour; access requirement; exact paths; tests; runtime evidence; configuration; technical maturity; commercial status; reachability; public availability; authoritative offer inclusion; limitations; permitted and prohibited wording; functional owner; last verification; freshness requirement; and re-verification trigger.

For every public claim or route finding, the contradiction register records: stable ID; route; source; visible claim or behaviour; claim type; evidence and gap; severity and risk; router, public, navigation, sitemap and indexing facts; content, CTA, persistence and destination facts; commercial approval; launch treatment; required authority; remediation phase; owner; last verification; and re-verification trigger.

## Scope exclusions

S1A may establish the evidence boundary and an entry gate for S1B. It does not approve any substantive proposition, commercial motion, price, offer, route or implementation decision belonging to S1B, S1C or S1D. No public-route repair, copywriting, price setting, entitlement change, CMS/table/API work, schema work or UI work is authorised by this audit.
