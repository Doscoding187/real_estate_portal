# DLE Product Vision

The Development Listing Engine is a guided commercial packaging engine.

Its job is to help a developer turn raw project information into a market-ready development package with speed, confidence, and clarity.

## Product Experience Standard

- The developer should feel guided, not trapped.
- The wizard should feel like packaging a commercial product, not filling a long admin form.
- Sale, rental, and auction flows should adapt copy, validation, pricing language, and next steps.
- Unit types should feel like the commercial inventory core.
- Public pages should visually prove the quality of the engine.
- Buyers, renters, investors, agents, and referrers should understand pricing, inventory, trust signals, and next steps.

## What Good Looks Like

- Developers know what is required and why it matters.
- Readiness feedback helps users complete market-critical information.
- Draft/edit/publish flows feel safe and predictable.
- Backend intelligence is visible in the wizard, public pages, search cards, unit cards, lead forms, dashboards, and distribution/referral views.
- The platform feels commercially serious without feeling heavy.

## Current Product Direction

The wizard shell should now make the active transaction engine visible throughout the packaging
flow. Sale, Rental, and Auction should not appear only as setup choices or conditional fields; each
lane should carry its own commercial language, pricing semantics, inventory signals, and public
output expectations as the developer works.

## What To Avoid

- Treating developments like single listings.
- Adding fields without user-facing meaning.
- Hiding sale/rent/auction intelligence only in backend helpers.
- Creating parallel legacy paths that increase drift.
- Redesigning UI before core save/resume/publish flows are proven.
